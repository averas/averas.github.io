INTRODUCTION

In this workshop you will analyze a dataset to determine which B2B (business) customers Tele2 should go after! Your job is to first explore the dataset using an interactive visulisation, and then, after you understand the data better, write an algorithm that does the job for you automatically. This is a very common approach in data-driven organisations like Tele2.

THE DATASET

The dataset represents roughly 4000 office locations in the greater Stockholm area. Each datapoint is one office location with some attributes. Your task will be to determine how we shold prioritisse these offices for marketing, based on the attributes. There is no right way of doing this, and reflecting over different angles is part of the workshop. You find the interactive visualisation here.

COMPANY REVENUE

All offices in the dataset belong to a company with a certain yearly income, or revenue. Arguably, companies with higher revenue may generate more busines for us. You can select the revenue mode in the visulisation and filter offices based on revenue in the filters to the right.

LEASED LINE COST

The black/purple markers on the map represent network hub sites where we can connect customers with leased lines. The cost of leased lines go up with distance, however, so we might prefer connecting offices that are close to our sites. Select the COST mode in the visualiastion and get a feel for how leased line cost goes up with distance to the nearest network site.

NUMBER OF OFFICES

Although each data point only repreents a single office there is information on the data points on how many offices the parent company has in total. An important business opportunity for communication providers like Tele2 is interconnecting offices with each other, therefore companies with many offices may constitute more attractive customers than companies with fewer offices.

OFFICE CLUSTERS

As mentioned above, leased line costs between our network hubs and customers affects the business case for any single office significantly. One way of approaching this is to lease lines between local customer deployments, which can be a much shorter distance. This means that "clusters" of offices with many nearby offices (regardless of company) are more attractive than isolated offices.

SUSTAINABILITY SCORE

Tele2 values sustainability very high and has several ongoing initatives to reduce environmental footprint and be in the forefont of sustainable buiness. As with our vendors, we could also target customerss that match our efforts in this area. Please discuss with your peers how and to what extent this could be incorporated into a buiness model.

EQUALITY SCORE

Another imortant area for Tele2 is equality. Like with sustainability this could also be a factor in whom we direct our business efforts towards. Discuss in your group if, why and how equality scoring could be part of a buiness model.
